# Case Study: The Jamtara Phishing & UPI Fraud Phenomenon

The town of Jamtara in Jharkhand gained international recognization as the "phishing capital of India," a reputation so significant that it inspired a popular Netflix web series. This case study explores that how scammers of jamtara manipulated people and di fraud.

### **What the Crime Is**

The primary crime is phishing combined with Financial fraud. Scammers played the people and manipulated them into taking their credential information like bank detail,upi pin and all of that.

### **How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)**

1. **Contact:** A scammer calls a victim, often posing as a bank official, KYC officer, or customer support representative.
2. **Creating Urgency:** They claim the victim's bank account or sim card has been deactivated and to activate that they needs to follow soem steps.
3. **The Hook:** The victim is asked to download a screen-sharing app (like AnyDesk) or click a link where their conformational data could be compromised.
4. **Exploitation:** By gaining access to the phone screen or by playing them they scam people into entering their upi pin.
5. **Theft:** The money is instantly transferred to the scammers account and they take out in form cash before any action through bank could be taken.
### **Who is Targeted & Consequences**

While anyone can be a target, scammers often seek out those with lower digital literacy, such as the elderly or first-time digital payment users. we should always be aware and if we see anyone being targeted we should quickly contack cybercrime police.
