# Case Study: The Jamtara Phishing & UPI Fraud Phenomenon

The town of Jamtara in Jharkhand gained international notoriety as the "phishing capital of India," a reputation so significant that it inspired a popular Netflix web series. This case study explores the UPI and Online Payment Fraud methods pioneered by these scammers to target unsuspecting citizens across the country.

### **What the Crime Is**

The primary crime is Phishing combined with Financial Fraud. Scammers use social engineering—the psychological manipulation of people into performing actions or divulging confidential information—to steal money directly from bank accounts via UPI (Unified Payments Interface).

### **How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)**

1. **Contact:** A scammer calls a victim, often posing as a bank official, KYC officer, or customer support representative.
2. **Creating Urgency:** They claim the victim's bank account or SIM card will be blocked unless they "verify" their details immediately.
3. **The Hook:** The victim is asked to download a screen-sharing app (like AnyDesk) or click a malicious link sent via SMS.
4. **Exploitation:** By gaining access to the phone screen or a "Collect Request" on a UPI app, the scammer tricks the victim into entering their UPI PIN.
5. **Theft:** The money is instantly transferred to a series of "mule" accounts to hide the trail.

### **Who is Targeted & Consequences**

While anyone can be a target, scammers often seek out those with lower digital literacy, such as the elderly or first-time digital payment users. The consequences are devastating, ranging from the total loss of life savings to severe emotional distress and a loss of trust in digital systems.
